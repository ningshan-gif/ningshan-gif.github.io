Caveman Papers is a project I built inside PennyCinematicUniverse around a simple idea: many great research papers never reach the people who would actually enjoy them because the format asks for too much attention up front. So for this project, I created animation agents that take a paper PDF and turn it into a short visual reel for people with shorter attention spans, while still preserving the core idea of the work.

The system treats a paper as raw narrative material. It reads the PDF, extracts the central argument, rewrites the content into a compact story, generates visual scenes, adds narration, and assembles the whole thing into a vertical short. Instead of asking viewers to commit to a dense paper before they know whether they care, Caveman Papers gives them an engaging on-ramp into the research first.

What I like about this project is that it treats science communication as an interface problem. The goal is not to replace the paper, but to make the first step easier: a fast, cinematic summary that can spark curiosity, lower the barrier to entry, and invite more people into technical ideas they might otherwise scroll past.
